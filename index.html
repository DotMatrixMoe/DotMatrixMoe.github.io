About

The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is the consequence of two plates diverging from each other, causing a gap between the two. Diverging for centuries, this ridge is about 620-932 miles wide! Explore with me, and find out: How did it form in the first place? Spoiler alert, it's complicated!

Geometry

The Divergent boundary is divided up against the South American Plate and the African plate. The tectonic plate includes the continent of South America as well as sizable region of the Atlantic Ocean. It expands more than 10,000 miles toward the African plate, which forms the entirety of the south of the ridge. Therefore the feature location of the South American and African plates are located at the Atlantic Ocean.

Fun-Facts!

Here are so pretty interesting facts about the Mid-Atlantic Ridge!

  • Because of the separation of the tectonic plates, it releases magma from the mantle. This also forms a new oceanintal crust in the ocean floor.
  • The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is mostly below water at about 87 degrees North 333km south of the North Pole to sub Antarctic Bourvet island at 54 degrees South.
  • This feature is known to be important due to crusts formed by the ridge. It helps keep a healthy amount of seafloor in the ocean.
